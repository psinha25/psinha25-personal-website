Projects

Most of my proudest technical projects are low level projects, as this is where my interests stand currently. That being said, I try to be well-rounded by participating in projects outside my core studies.

Micro AMDC

Designed a PCB using Altium to control power electronics for Professor Severson's research. Currently assembling the board by hand and writing firmware for the STM32H743 DSP and iCE Lattice FPGA.

5 Stage Pipelined CPU

Built a 5 stage pipelined (IF, ID, EX, MEM, WB) CPU written in Verilog for ECE 552 at UW-Madison. Decided to go one step further and integrate a 2-way set associate cache for the separate instruction and data memory.

Logic Analyzer

Desined a 5-channel logic analyzer used for debugging physical connections between a MCU and peripherals (i.e. acclerometers). Established edge, level, SPI, and UART based triggering to enable sampling of data from the channels.

Football Stars

Programmed a game in C on an ARM Cortex-m4 MCU. Wrote drivers for ADC, GPIO, UART/SPI/I2C, and timers. Utilized push buttons, LCD screen, EEPROM, PS2 joystick, and LEDs to create the entire game.

DBMS Architecture

Created the underlying components of a DBMS. Specifically, wrote the buffer manager and a B+tree index on integer keys. Written in C++. Project for CS 564 at UW-Madison.

Case Competitions

Participated in 4 consulting and business focused case competitions during my time at UW-Madison. Companies include Intuit (2x), Accenture, and ULine. Won all 4 competitions.
